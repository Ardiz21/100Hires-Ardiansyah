## Post 1: The Two Psychological Principles Behind Cold Email
**Date collected:** June 2026
**Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_your-cold-email-outbound-in-general-relies-activity-7372241459581853696-A2-t?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAACjYR14B6Z6VdxZqkrxBVc3qcnhIVmTzIfA

**Summary:** Allred argues cold outreach success rests on two psychological principles: Cognitive Load Theory and Reciprocity. On cognitive load, he claims readers need to understand a message in under roughly nine seconds, and that even a single long sentence measurably reduces reply rates; his prescription is short words, short sentences, short paragraphs, and one idea per email. On reciprocity, he distinguishes relevance (what targeted ads already do) from genuinely compelling a reader to respond, arguing that a prospect needs to feel a message was specifically written for them and that real effort went into it before they feel obligated to reply. He frames irrelevant, low-effort personalization and account-based prospecting through this same reciprocity lens.

**Key takeaway for playbook:** Reply rates depend on minimizing reading friction (short, simple structure) and signaling genuine personalized effort (triggering reciprocity), rather than relying on relevance alone, which prospects already get from ads.
