## Post 1: Pitching vs. Poking
**Date collected:** June 2026
**Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_imagine-youre-cold-calling-a-car-dealership-activity-7471996971323510784-ZINX?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAACjYR14B6Z6VdxZqkrxBVc3qcnhIVmTzIfA

**Summary:** Braun contrasts two cold outreach approaches using a car dealership example. "Pitching" means stating conclusions and data points at the prospect (e.g., citing stats about response times and automation benefits), which puts the seller in control of the conversation. "Poking" means presenting the same information as an observation or question, prompting the buyer to reflect and arrive at the realization themselves (e.g., asking how quickly leads actually get followed up on at night). His core argument: prospects rarely argue with their own conclusions, so questions that invite self-examination outperform direct pitches.

**Key takeaway for playbook:** Effective cold outreach reframes facts as curiosity-provoking questions rather than as direct statements. Letting the buyer arrive at the problem themselves is more persuasive than explaining the problem to them.
