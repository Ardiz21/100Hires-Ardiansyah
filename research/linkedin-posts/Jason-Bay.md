## Post 1: The Real Purpose of the First Line
**Date collected:** June 2026
**Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jasondbay_i-disagree-that-the-first-line-of-a-cold-activity-7468678070321770497-EtBb?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAACjYR14B6Z6VdxZqkrxBVc3qcnhIVmTzIfA    

**Summary:** Bay challenges the common advice that a cold email's first line exists only to get the reader to the second line. Citing reading-behavior research (eye-tracking and content studies), he notes that most readers scan in an F-shape, focus on the left side of text, and often skip straight to the call-to-action. He argues the first line actually serves two functions: driving the open (since it appears in inbox preview text) and building urgency by referencing something timely and specific to the buyer's business or industry. He outlines three opener types: a trigger-based opener referencing a recent company event, a tension-based opener naming a likely industry problem, and a trust-based opener using social proof from a similar company.

**Key takeaway for playbook:** The first line of a cold email should be optimized for two jobs simultaneously, driving the open via preview text and creating urgency via relevance, rather than purely as a hook into the rest of the email. Three reusable opener types: trigger, tension, and trust.
